Temporal Encoding Strategies for Energy Time Series Prediction

Time Series Machine Learning Energy
Aayam Bansal et al.
View Paper

In contemporary power systems, energy consumption prediction plays a crucial role in maintaining grid stability and resource allocation enabling power companies to minimize energy waste and avoid overloading the grid. While there are several research works on energy optimization, they often fail to address the complexities of real-time fluctuations and the cyclic pattern of energy consumption.

This work proposes a novel approach to enhance the accuracy of predictive models by employing sinusoidal encoding on periodic features of time-series data. The results demonstrated a 12.6% improvement of Root Mean Squared Error (from 0.5497 to 0.4802) and a 7.8% increase in the R² score (from 0.7530 to 0.8118), indicating that the proposed encoding better captures the cyclic nature of temporal patterns than traditional methods.

Early Detection of Mental Health Crises through Social Media Analysis

Mental Health NLP Social Media
Aayam Bansal et al.
View Paper

Mental health crises represent serious challenges for public health systems everywhere. Early detection and intervention is crucial to mitigate the impact of these crises on individuals and communities. This paper collected social media posts from 10,000 users and analyzed the linguistic markers associated with changes in mental health status over a 12-month period.

Using a BERT-LSTM model, our NLP model was able to identify users with deteriorating mental health (≡ 83%). The most significant linguistic cues were greater rates of first-person singular pronouns, negative emotion words and different levels of sentence complexity.

Linguistic Complexity and Socio-cultural Patterns in Hip-Hop Lyrics

Linguistics Cultural Computing Music
Aayam Bansal et al.
View Paper

Rap music, from its roots in cultural resistance and self-expression, has transformed into a global phenomenon appreciated for its intricate lyricism and strong commentary. This study delves into the linguistic, thematic, and stylistic aspects of rap lyrics through computational and interpretive lenses.

With a dataset of lyrics from famous rappers across several decades, we measure linguistic complexity, sentiment polarity, and thematic diversity. This paper also explains the ways in which social-political contexts and individual experience influence rap art, with regards to its adaptability and the mastery language itself holds for its artists.

Adaptive Pedestrian Crossing System Using AI and Computer Vision

Computer Vision Smart Cities Transport
Aayam Bansal et al.
View Paper

With the growth of urbanization, an increasing pedestrian and vehicular flow has posed numerous challenges. The application of fixed-time pedestrian crossing systems often fails in conventional techniques for the optimization of safety and efficiency. A new pedestrian crossing system, adaptive in nature, with AI and computer vision as its driving forces, changes the signal timings with real-time changes in pedestrian density and environmental conditions.

Testing with simulation showed that this system reduced overall average pedestrian wait time by 35%, vehicle delays by 20%, and increased safe crossings by 15% compared with fixed-time systems. Real-world testing supports this assertion, with the system showing a reduction in pedestrian wait times as high as 28%.

Emotional Resonance in AI-Generated Music for Stress Therapy in Marginalized Communities

Music AI Therapy Healthcare
Aayam Bansal et al.
View Paper

This paper explored the use of AI to reflect on the music that resonates with the beliefs of certain communities and can be cultivated in order to create emotionally resonant cultural embeds, specifically within marginalized communities. The adaptability of AI-generated music better fits the cultural context of a person which could enhance the emotional connection and efficacy of therapy.

This paper employs both a theoretical analysis of AI music techniques, emotional resonance and culturally adaptive therapies present in the literature to argue that culturally specific music can enhance the therapeutic effect for marginalized groups, serving as an accessible and individually tailored option to tackle chronic stress.
